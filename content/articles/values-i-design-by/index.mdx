<h2 id="inventory">§1 · An inventory, not a manifesto</h2>

The word "values" has a manifesto smell to it. I don't mean it that way here. I mean the things I keep coming back to when a design gets hard — the arguments I have with myself at 11pm when the easy answer is in front of me and I'm pretty sure it's wrong.

This is a working list. It'll be different next quarter.

<h2 id="flexibility">§2 · What flexibility actually costs</h2>

Most teams say they value flexibility. Few of them pay for it. Flexibility costs decisions — every place the design could have hardened but didn't. It's expensive to live with and frustrating to demo.

<PullQuote attribution="a draft from March">
  Flexibility is what you leave room for on purpose, not what you failed to decide.
</PullQuote>

<h2 id="protect">§3 · What I’d protect, and why</h2>

If I were a leader — which I am not, yet — the thing I'd protect is the team's ability to change its mind without shame. Not decisions. Not roadmaps. The *muscle*.

I protect it in myself first because it's the only place I have authority.
