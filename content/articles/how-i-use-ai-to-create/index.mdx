<h2 id="codebase">§1 · Into the codebase</h2>

For a while I worked at an early-stage startup where I was wired directly into the codebase. I shipped to staging myself; my changes got merged to main every week. The team was all engineers — and they wanted a designer. Not despite AI. Because of it. The hands that could move between the two modes were the bottleneck, not either mode alone.

You have to put yourself in an environment like that to feel the answer. Reading about it isn't the same.

<h2 id="question">§2 · A question I keep thinking about</h2>

Someone once asked me, very seriously: *what do you think the value of designers should be, then?*

The question has been in my head ever since.

<PullQuote attribution="a note from around then">
  The worry isn't "AI replaces designers." It's "designers don't step into the seat AI leaves open."
</PullQuote>

<h2 id="stop">§3 · It doesn't stop</h2>

Once I spent enough time collaborating with Claude, the limits became specific.

**It doesn't stop.** Prompt Claude to *"act as a world-class designer, critique the work, and then give suggestions based on the critique"* and it will keep going. It will critique, suggest, critique the suggestion, suggest again. Someone still has to decide *enough*. That someone is still me.
