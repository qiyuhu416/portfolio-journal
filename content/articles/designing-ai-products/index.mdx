<p>Since when did "humans" become a frequent word?</p>

<p>
    Pre-AI, we didn’t say “humans” this much, we were just... in it.
    But once AI entered the room, we started seeing ourselves from the outside.

Designing AI is not always about making the AI smarter, it's always mapping the UX to the current tech capability. Sharing some reflections from my work in academia and industry:

<h2 id="errors">§1 · Designing for errors  <span style={{ color: 'var(--ink-3)', fontWeight: 400 }}>(if AI has easy inferences)</span></h2>
<h3 id="easy-inference">Case study: phone call agent prototype to elicit errors</h3>

<p>AI is never perfect (yet). Please.</p>

<p>Maybe part of the reason was AI was trained based on human data, and humans are not that perfect either.</p>


<Sidebar label="Welcome to the adult world">
  <ul style={{ margin: 0, paddingLeft: 0, listStyle: 'none', lineHeight: 1.7 }}>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}>
      what one thinks <b>!=</b> what one says
    </li>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 8 }}>
      what one says <b>!=</b> what one does
    </li>
    <li style={{ marginBottom: 0 }}>
      what one wants to express <b>!=</b> what others perceive
    </li>
  </ul>
</Sidebar>

<p>
Therefore, the design focus should be: 
</p>

<p>
<strong>Where will it fail? Which failures are okay? How do we handle them?</strong>
</p>

<p>
I was exploring this solution for a non-profit to address their "too many repetitive questions but not enough staff" painpoint.
The goal was not really to prove “AI works.”
It was to surface what could go wrong before it actually goes wrong.
</p>


<p>
Solo prototyper, some rule-based logic from client interviews, 2 days to finish, 1 hour to test, but many interesting findings:
</p>

<h2 id="relationship">§2 · Designing the relationship  <span style={{ color: 'var(--ink-3)', fontWeight: 400 }}>(if AI has hard inferences)</span></h2>
<h3 id="hard-inference">Case study: elder care Bot (research @AI CARING)</h3>


<p>
If we look at the evolution of HCI, it has always been about relationships.
</p>

<p>
humans adapt to machines (terminal) → machines adapt to humans (GUI) → humans & machines work together (AI agents)
</p>

<Aside>
Interestingly we’re now back to terminal again (hello, Claude Code)

<p>We design human–AI relationships by borrowing from human–human relationships.</p>

<p>
<strong>BUT HUMANS ARE COMPLICATED.</strong> And human–human relationships are even more complicated.
So one overarching research question:
</p>

<Takeaway label="02">In high-inference scenarios, trust also means "I am not capable of doing this"</Takeaway>



In eldercare, the trickiest part is when the older adult's health declines — that changes a lot of things. The bot is more than just a messenger; it's a party that knows information from both sides and can talk to both. But the elder might not want the bot to tell everything to their caregiver.

  <summary style={{

    "Affiliation" in human-robot interaction
  </summary>

  <div style={{ marginTop: 14, fontSize: 16, lineHeight: 1.6, color: 'var(--ink-2)' }}>
    <p style={{ marginTop: 0 }}>
      In social science (Stivers et al., 2011; Lee & Tanaka, 2016), <em>affiliation</em> is the affective stance of being on someone's side, displaying empathy, matching their preference, cooperating. It's distinct from <em>alignment</em>, which is the structural level of cooperation (just facilitating the action).
    </p>
    <p>
      Affiliation can be approach-based (love, secure attachment, intimacy) or avoidance-based (laughing off tension, fearing rejection, fearing disapproval). Even when humans show affiliation, there's often avoidance underneath.
    </p>
    <p>
      When we tried to define what "affiliation" should mean for the bot, we had six possible goals on the table:
    </p>
    <ul style={{ paddingLeft: 22 }}>
      <li>instructions</li>
      <li>expressed intentions</li>
      <li>revealed preferences (what behavior reveals one prefers)</li>
      <li>informed preferences (what one would want if rational and informed)</li>
      <li>interests</li>
      <li>value (what is moral)</li>
    </ul>
    <p style={{ marginBottom: 0 }}>
      We narrowed to <em>"best interest"</em>, broad enough to cover the scenarios, specific enough to actually design around.
    </p>
  </div>

</details>
