<h2 id="curiosity">§2 · That mindset of having fun</h2>

Before going deeper, I think it's important to ask: why are we learning AI.

No need to have a clear answer, but acknowledging the real driving force is important.

I've chased AI tools because of FOMO, and I knew it was because of FOMO.


So many many many people have mentioned "you need to find your real passion for work." I know the importance, but passion is something you can't have if this is just not your passion.
For a long time I thought I might just be too idealistic, until I saw people who are genuinely passionate about their work, and said "I would still want to do this job even if I was not getting paid" (Samar K). (link in quote).

The "Palo Alto" video I created was purely for fun, even if it is not getting used anywhere, but it is just fun to create something that I WANT to create.

I guess that was why I was willing to spend the whole day to explore a new tool just to create it, because using the tool now has a goal.

Now that vibe coding and many other tools have made creation much easier, I can create more, if I want.

To my future self & whoever is reading this, I hope I can remember that feeling of having fun, especially when AI comes out and many people were creating.


<h2 id="prompts">§3 · So, what to create?</h2>

It is very easy to duplicate a similar effect.

<h2 id="question">§4 · It needs people</h2>

It is a design question: who you wanna be with / how you would want to design your social circle.

There are times I feel so FOMO with this AI tool and that AI tool.
I've joined communities that other people have initiated, and also initiated community that I would want to be in.

This whole article was something I shared during one of the sharing sessions with the team. I hope me and my friends can all have real fun with what they are doing.
