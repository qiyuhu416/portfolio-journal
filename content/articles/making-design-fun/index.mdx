<h2 id="caltrain">§1 · The Caltrain moment</h2>

One day I was on a Caltrain heading into Palo Alto. I had a word stuck in my head and wanted to animate it — just a small playful thing, letters doing letter things. So I opened After Effects.

It took me almost the whole day. It mostly worked.

<Figure fig="01" placeholder alt="After Effects word animation" caption="The animation I made. Screen recording goes here." />

A few months later the vibe-coding tools got good, and the same idea started taking minutes instead of a day.

<h2 id="prompts">§2 · One block of prompts</h2>

Not long after, I saw a post of someone using hand gestures to copy a move from a magic movie. It looked complicated. Then I scrolled into the comments and saw: *it was one block of prompts*.

I tried it. And then I started asking: if hand gesture works, what else works?

In user research I already think this way — you don't only ask people to *say* things, you watch where their eyes go, what they reach for, how long they pause. Eye tracking, voice, camera, keyboard. A menu of inputs.

So I started playing with the same menu, but for making:

<Figure fig="03" placeholder alt="Hand gesture experiment" caption="Hand gesture piece #2 — this time steering a small drawing." />

The more I tried, the more I felt technology as something that could *let me do the thing*, rather than stand between me and it.

<h2 id="question">§3 · The question that stuck</h2>

Which left me with a question that keeps following me around:

<PullQuote attribution="the question, underlined">
  What else have I been doing the hard way for years, only because the easier way wasn't available yet?
</PullQuote>

Presentations, for one. People reached for slides because other formats were too slow to build, and writing alone couldn't carry the visual. But a website is faster than a deck now — and honestly more fun to make. The communication goal hasn't changed. The tool just doesn't need to be the same one.

Birthday cards, too. Same logic. I think I'll keep looking for the next one.
