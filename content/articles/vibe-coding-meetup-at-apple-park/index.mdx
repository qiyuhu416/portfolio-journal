<h2 id="why">§ Why</h2>
<p>I love connecting people. I want my friends to be friends with each other.</p>

I don't want work to be the laptop-and-the-meeting loop, eight hours of getting-things-done and then going home.

So I joined a lot of channels at Apple. Japanese language. Tennis. AI. Whatever small Slack room had people gathering around something that wasn't the job. Most of the people I'm glad I met, I met through those channels, not through the work I was hired to do.

<Figure fig="01" placeholder alt="Channels and coffee chats" caption="A collage of the channels, screenshots, and the coffees that came out of them." />

It turns out *"have fun at work"* is a design choice you make on yourself.

<h2 id="why">§ Why</h2>

In October 2025, I noticed vibe coding was such a powerful tool but mainly engineers were using it not designers. In the meantime, there are still some designers scattered around different teams exploring it.
Instead of me reaching out to each individual designers like this, why not just gathering people together. 
So I host my first cross-team vibe coding session. 
<Figure fig="01" placeholder alt="Apple Park meetup" caption="Lunch, a round of introductions, someone's laptop open in the middle of the table." />
After that, more...
Also interesting to see the type of event changes throughout the progress, as a way o reflect my thoughts on AI


It is interesting to step out of my own team to talk with people approaching the same problem but in different context.
It is also interesting to see "everyone is really humans" - there are people from different background and titles, but we are all equally curious and friendly. 
People really matters to me, and I am grateful for the people I've been able to met through the job.
