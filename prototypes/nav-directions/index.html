Four directions for the quadrant nav

Each prototype shows the same scene: user is reading Qiyu · Do (top-right), the three idle corners belong to the mirror, attention, and work. Compare how each treats focus, affordance, and color.
Direction A · Editorial

Printed page in a quiet room

Active quadrant feels like paper on a table. Idle corners carry tiny mono running headers instead of tint. Typography carries navigation; color retreats to content only.
Direction B · Wayfinding

Museum floor plan

Each idle corner gets a directional sign — an arrow and a label — pointing to that section. Like walking through gallery rooms. Zero background colors.
Direction C · Inversion

Negative image

Active quadrant stays cream with a subtle inset shadow. The three idle corners become a hair lighter. No tints. Your instinct, minimally applied.
Direction D · Engraving

Line work

Each quadrant carries a different hairline pattern at ~4% — dots, dashes, rules, cross-hatch. Active is blank paper. Identity via texture, not color.
Direction E · Camera

Zoom navigation

Scroll starts on an overview of all four quadrants. Click one to zoom in; scroll up at the top (or ESC) to zoom back out. No tour — the user chooses the path, and the camera does the explaining.
